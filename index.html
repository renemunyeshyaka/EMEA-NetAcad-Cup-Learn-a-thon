Competition Overview

The EMEA NetAcad Cup Learn-a-thon brings together networking enthusiasts from across the region to compete, learn, and grow their IT skills.

What is the NetAcad Cup Learn-a-thon?

The NetAcad Cup Learn-a-thon is an exciting competition organized by Cisco Networking Academy for students across Europe, Middle East, and Africa. Participants enroll in specialized courses, complete modules, and compete on skill-based challenges.

This year's competition includes both the Learn-a-thon (learning-focused) and Capture the Flag (hands-on security challenges) components. Participants will gain hands-on experience with cutting-edge technologies while competing for recognition and prizes.

The competition is open to all University of Rwanda students enrolled in Cisco Networking Academy courses. No prior experience is required for introductory courses.

Hands-on cybersecurity competition following the Learn-a-thon

About Capture the Flag Competition

Capture the Flag (CTF) is a hands-on cybersecurity competition where participants solve security challenges in various categories such as cryptography, web exploitation, reverse engineering, and forensics.

This exciting component of the NetAcad Cup tests practical security skills in a competitive environment. Teams work together to "capture flags" by solving complex security challenges.

The CTF competition is open to qualifying academies from the Learn-a-thon phase. Teams must register and prepare for both regional group phases and the grand finale.
